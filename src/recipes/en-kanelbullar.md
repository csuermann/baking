Swedish kanelbullar are the original behind all cinnamon roll variations — moist, buttery, and with the unmistakable scent of cinnamon and a hint of cocoa. The soft enriched dough is rolled into a large rectangle, spread with cinnamon butter, folded, then cut into strips that are individually twisted and coiled. The secret is the double proof, which gives the dough its airy lightness.

## Steps

### Dissolve the yeast

duration: 5

Pour **3–4 tbsp of the lukewarm milk** into a small bowl, crumble in the fresh yeast, and stir with a fork until fully dissolved.

Warm the remaining milk with the butter over **low heat** until the butter has just melted — the mixture should be **warm but not hot** (max. 37 °C / 99 °F).

### Knead the dough

duration: 10

Combine flour, sugar, and salt in a large bowl. Add the yeast mixture and the milk-butter mixture. Knead everything **for about 10 minutes** vigorously until you have a **smooth, elastic dough** that pulls away from the sides of the bowl.

### First proof

duration: 60-75
passive: true

Shape the dough into a ball, cover with cling film and a tea towel. Leave to rise in a **warm spot for about 1 hour** until the volume has **clearly doubled**. The dough is ready when a fingertip impression springs back slowly.

### Prepare the filling and roll out the dough

duration: 15

Stir the butter, cinnamon sugar, cocoa powder, and optional cardamom together into a smooth paste. Give the dough a brief knead, then roll it out on a lightly floured surface into a **rectangle roughly 40 cm × (2 × number of buns) cm**. Spread the cinnamon butter evenly across the entire surface.

### Shape the rolls

duration: 15

Fold the dough like a **letter** (fold the bottom third up, fold the top third down over it). Cut the folded dough crosswise into **strips about 2 cm wide**. Give each strip **one gentle twist**, then coil it into a spiral and place it end-side down on a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Leave **some space** between them — they will puff up.

### Second proof

duration: 30-45
passive: true

Cover the rolls loosely with a tea towel and leave to rise for **30–45 minutes** until they have visibly puffed up and feel soft and airy to the touch. At the same time, preheat the oven to **200 °C / 390 °F** (top and bottom heat).

### Bake

duration: 13-15

Brush the rolls with beaten egg and sprinkle with **pearl sugar**. Bake in the preheated oven for **13–15 minutes** until **golden brown**. Don't overbake — they should still be soft inside. Cool on a wire rack for a few minutes and **enjoy while fresh**.
