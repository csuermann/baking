A purist whole rye sourdough with just four ingredients. The short, warm proof of **4–7 hours at 27°C / 81°F** makes it possible to bake in a single day. The dough develops its flavour quickly and reliably. The result is a hearty, surprisingly open-crumbed loaf with a crunchy crust that stays fresh for days.

## Steps

### Mix and tin the dough

duration: 10

Weigh all ingredients into a bowl and mix by hand or with a sturdy spoon until fully combined into a uniform, sticky mass — no dry flour should remain visible. Work in the sunflower seeds evenly.

Transfer the dough into a greased or parchment-lined loaf tin (approx. **22 × 10 × 9 cm / 9 × 4 × 3.5 in**). Smooth the surface with wet hands or a wet dough scraper. Cover with a lid, cling film, or a bag to prevent drying out.

**Tip for a rustic surface:** Dust the surface with flour and leave the tin uncovered for the first 2 hours — this encourages attractive cracks to form as the dough rises.

### Bulk proof at 27°C

duration: 240-420
passive: true

Leave the dough to ripen for **4–7 hours at 27°C / 81°F**. Don't touch it, don't adjust it.

The dough is ready to bake when:

- **many cracks** are visible on the floured surface
- the volume has **roughly doubled**
- the dough sits **just below the rim** of the tin
- the surface is **slightly domed**

### Bake

duration: 70

Preheat the oven to **250°C / 480°F** (top and bottom heat). Place the tin in the hot oven, immediately reduce the temperature to **200°C / 390°F**, and bake for **65–70 minutes** until the crust is a deep dark brown and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

### Cool

duration: 60
passive: true

Turn the loaf out of the tin immediately and cool on a wire rack for at least **1 hour** — longer is better. Rye bread only slices cleanly once it has cooled completely.
