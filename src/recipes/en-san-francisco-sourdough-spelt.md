A three-stage spelt sourdough built around a stiff wheat starter. The **tangzhong** gelatinises a portion of the flour, keeping the crumb moist and fresh for days. The **autolyse** develops the gluten network gently before the final dough is brought together. An **overnight cold proof** slows fermentation, deepens the flavour and firms up the loaf — making it easy to score straight from the fridge. Baked in a covered Dutch oven for a blistered, open crust with good bloom.

## Steps

### Build the levain

duration: 10

Mix the water (40°C), stiff wheat starter and spelt flour until no dry flour remains. Target temperature: **28°C**. Cover and leave at 28°C.

### Make the tangzhong

duration: 5

Whisk the water and spelt flour together until smooth and lump-free. Heat over medium, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens to a pudding-like paste. Transfer to a bowl, press cling film **directly onto the surface** and leave to cool to room temperature.

### Ripen the levain

duration: 120-180

Leave the levain to ripen at **28°C for 2–3 hours**. It is ready when the volume has visibly increased, the surface shows bubbles and the aroma is pleasantly sour. The tangzhong will have cooled down in the meantime.

### Mix autolyse and rest

duration: 35

Combine the water (30°C), spelt flour and the entire cooled tangzhong (~95g) and mix until a firm, homogeneous dough forms — it will feel intentionally dry; the bassinage comes later. Target temperature: **24°C**. Cover and rest for **30 minutes** at 24°C.

### Mix and knead final dough

duration: 15

Add all the ripe levain, salt and ascorbic acid to the autolyse dough. Knead until the dough pulls cleanly from the bowl and has built up some tension (about 8–10 minutes). Then work in the **bassinage** a tablespoon at a time: add a little water, knead it in fully before adding the next portion. The dough should stay smooth and medium-firm. Finish with a **windowpane test**: stretch a small piece — it should thin to a translucent membrane without tearing. Target temperature: **26°C**.

### Bulk fermentation with stretch & fold

duration: 180-240

Place the dough in a lightly oiled container, cover and ferment at **24°C** until it has grown to 1.5–2× its original volume (about 3–3.5 hours). During the first 2 hours **stretch and fold four times**:

- **30 min:** 1st stretch & fold
- **60 min:** 2nd stretch & fold
- **90 min:** 3rd stretch & fold
- **120 min:** 4th stretch & fold

### Shape

duration: 5

Gently turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Fold it loosely into a round, then use your hands to drag it toward you and build surface tension into a tight boule.

### Cold proof

duration: 480-960

Place the shaped loaf seam-side up in a banneton dusted with rye or rice flour. Cover with cling film or a shower cap and proof **overnight in the refrigerator at 4–6°C** (8–16 hours). The loaf should have visibly grown and spring back slowly when gently pressed.

### Score and bake covered

duration: 22

Preheat the oven with the Dutch oven inside to **250°C / 480°F** (at least 45 minutes). Take the loaf out of the refrigerator only just before baking.

Turn the cold loaf out onto a sheet of baking paper (seam side now down). Score the surface with a razor blade or sharp knife at a **45° angle** — the cold, firm dough scores cleanly and holds the cut.

Lower the loaf and paper into the hot Dutch oven and put the lid on. After **5 minutes** reduce the temperature to **220°C / 425°F**. Bake covered for **20 minutes** total.

### Bake uncovered

duration: 35

Remove the lid and bake for a further **35 minutes** until deep golden brown. Lift the loaf onto a wire rack — it should sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

### Cool

duration: 60

Rest the loaf on the wire rack for at least **1 hour** before slicing. The crumb continues to set as it cools.
